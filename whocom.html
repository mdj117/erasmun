Topic: Ensuring the equitable distribution of healthcare resources

Ensuring the equitable distribution of healthcare resources is an ever-present issue, especially in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the implications of equitable distribution have a wider scope - access to healthcare is a human right as stated by the WHO Constitution (1946). Equity, by definition, means equal distribution of resources among parties while taking into account their needs. UN secretary general, António Guterres, called the COVID-19 vaccine distribution the greatest current moral test for the global community. The fight against the pandemic, however, can only be won by a collective effort. Indeed, if the virus spreads and constantly mutates in unvaccinated populations, vaccinated populations will not be protected. The only way out is ensuring access of healthcare resources for everyone. The committee aims to tackle challenges such as: The equitable distribution of vaccines and other healthcare resources; Ensuring access to these healthcare recourses by the stateless and displaced people (refugees, indigenous populations, people in conflict zones…); The ethics of medical patents and their impact on the sharing of knowledge and recourses.
