Topic: Revaluating the rules of Schengen

In 1995, the Schengen Agreement (signed a decade earlier) came to power. This unprecedented treaty largely removed the internal border checks between the parties of the agreement and ultimately led to an even stronger unification between the European Union member states. Today, over 26 years have passed since the creation of the Schengen Area, and during that time, it has been challenged by new crises. The 2015 immigrant crisis, Brexit, and the COVID-19 pandemic all resulted in several states reinstating the border controls or closing their border completely. A time has now come to revaluate the rules of the treaty. This committee will aim to rewrite and update the rules governing the Schengen Area: What should be the requirements of entering the Schengen Area? Should the criteria for all states willing to enter Schengen be re-evaluated? Should there be new measures aiming to further prevent the smuggling of people, weapons, and illegal substances through the internal borders of Schengen? What about the unified tariffs on imports of goods from outside? Should there be a framework for states willing to leave the Schengen in the future?
