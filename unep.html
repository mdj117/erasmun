Topic: Developing a framework for sustainable energy transition

With the increasing threat of climate change caused by the emissions of greenhouse gases, a transition towards sustainable energy production is of key importance. In the Paris Agreement, most of the UN countries agreed to take the necessary precautions to limit the anthropogenic warming to 1.5Â°C relative to the pre-industrial levels. Six years later, however, one can observe that most countries are far behind the set goals. There is a considerable need for updating current climate agreements and finding the means to ensure that they are being followed. Questions regarding energy sources and possible transition strategies lead to heavy debates: Should a state allocate significant budget to research and development of the renewable energy sources? Or should a state spend that budget on constructing wind and solar farms with existing technology instead? Are renewable energy sources always sustainable in the long run? What is the role of the nuclear energy in the transition? How to involve and convince the public of the importance and urgency of the topic? What will be the economic and social impacts of each possible strategy?
